## Example 1: Clan of Xymox (Artist, P1)

Clan of Xymox emerged from Nijmegen, Netherlands, in 1981 — Ronny Moorings (vocals, guitar, electronics) and Anka Wolbert (bass, vocals) building skeletal darkwave from drum machines, analog synthesizers, and Moorings' baritone, which always sounded like it was arriving from the far end of an empty corridor. Their self-titled debut (1985, 4AD) and Medusa (1986, 4AD) defined a sound that was precisely calibrated between coldwave austerity and something approaching pop melody — songs that could have been hits if they hadn't been recorded in the dark.

The 4AD years remain the reference point: Ivo Watts-Russell's label context, alongside Cocteau Twins and Dead Can Dance, positioned Xymox as part of a scene that valued atmosphere over energy. Moorings continued the project through lineup changes and decades of releases, the analog warmth of the early records gradually giving way to digital production without ever fully abandoning the nocturnal register that made the first records unmistakable.

MEMBERS:
- Ronny Moorings — vocals, guitar, electronics (1981–present), founder
- Anka Wolbert — bass, vocals (1981–1990)
- Pieter Nooten — keyboards (1981–1989)
- Mojca Zugna — bass, vocals (1999–present)

## Example 2: Martial Canterel (Artist, P2)

Sean McBride records as Martial Canterel with the economy of a technician: one voice, one synthesizer, one drum machine. Since 2004, the New York-based project has produced minimal synth that strips the genre to its circuit-board essentials — monophonic bass sequences, metronomic rhythm, and McBride's flat, affectless vocals delivered with the emotional temperature of a readout. The recordings for Wierd Records and Dais carry the cold precision of early DAF and the ghost of Silicon Teens, rebuilt for basements rather than dance floors.

MEMBERS:
- Sean McBride — vocals, synthesizers, drum machines (2004–present)

## Example 3: Linea Aspera (Artist, P3)

Alison Lewis (vocals, synthesizer) and Ryan Ambridge (synthesizer, drum machine) operated Linea Aspera for one self-titled album (2012, Dark Entries) and a handful of singles before dissolving. That brief output — precise, nocturnal, and disciplined in its restraint — became a reference point for the minimal synth revival of the 2010s. Lewis' voice, controlled and deliberately unexpressive, sat atop Ambridge's analog sequences like text on a blank page: readable precisely because there was nothing else competing for attention.

MEMBERS:
- Alison Lewis — vocals, synthesizer (2011–2013)
- Ryan Ambridge — synthesizer, drum machine (2011–2013)

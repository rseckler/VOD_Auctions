# PRESS ORG EXAMPLES — 2 different types

---

## Example 1: Industrial News (Press, P2 — Fanzine, scene documentation)

Industrial News was the connective tissue of the early British industrial scene. Published from London in the early 1980s, the fanzine covered the network of artists, labels, and cassette operations that constituted the post-Throbbing Gristle underground — reviews of releases that no other publication acknowledged existed, interviews conducted through mail correspondence, and classified ads that functioned as the scene's marketplace before the internet.

The publication's format — photocopied, stapled, distributed through the same postal networks as the music it covered — was inseparable from its content. Industrial News didn't report on the cassette underground from outside. It was the cassette underground, rendered as text.

---

## Example 2: Re:Records Quarterly (Press, P1 — Critical journal, curated perspective)

Chris Cutler's Re:Records Quarterly (later renamed The Organ of Corti) operated from London as something rarer than a music magazine: a critical journal with a consistent editorial position. Published alongside the Recommended Records label and distribution network from 1981, the Quarterly provided long-form essays, artist manifestos, label profiles, and philosophical arguments about the economics and aesthetics of independent music.

Where fanzines documented scenes, the Quarterly argued for positions: self-distribution as political practice, improvisation as compositional methodology, the LP as a format with specific structural implications. Cutler's editorial voice — informed by Marxist cultural theory and practical experience as a working musician (Henry Cow, Art Bears) — gave the publication a coherence rare in music journalism. The Quarterly was partisan, deliberate, and uninterested in coverage for its own sake.

# BANNED PHRASES — Writer Agent must never use these

## Category 1: "I don't know" padding
- "Limited documentation exists regarding"
- "Though limited documentation survives"
- "Limited information is available about"
- "The specifics of their broader discography remain unclear"
- "Further details about the project remain undocumented"

## Category 2: Generic context filler
- "broader landscape of"
- "broader context of"
- "within the broader"
- "part of the broader independent music infrastructure"
- "consistent with underground music distribution practices"
- "formative period"
- "during a crucial period in"
- "during this pivotal era"
- "reflects the aesthetics and DIY ethos"

## Category 3: AI writing tics
- "fundamentally shaped"
- "fundamentally transformed"
- "characterized by"
- "it's worth noting"
- "it is worth mentioning"
- "delve into"
- "sonic exploration"
- "boundary-pushing"
- "sonic landscape"
- "tapestry of sound"
- "a unique blend of"
- "exploring the boundaries of"
- "pushing the envelope"
- "blurring the lines between"
- "a testament to"

## Category 4: Generic openings (never start with these)
- "[Name] is a [type] known for"
- "[Name] is a [nationality] [type] that"
- "[Name] was a [type] formed in"
- "[Name] is an obscure project associated with"
- "[Name] represents an obscure entry in"

## Category 5: Empty adjectives (use specific ones instead)
- "innovative" (say what the innovation was)
- "influential" (say who was influenced)
- "prolific" (give a number)
- "unique" (describe what makes it different)
- "groundbreaking" (describe what ground was broken)
- "seminal" (name what it seeded)
- "iconic" (overused, avoid entirely)

## Category 6: Filler conclusions
- "contributing to the experimental music community"
- "representing an important segment of"
- "serving enthusiasts of avant-garde"
- "continues to inspire"
- "remains relevant to this day"
- "leaving an indelible mark on"

## Rule: If you don't have information, write LESS — not more.
## A 2-sentence factual description beats a 5-sentence apology.
